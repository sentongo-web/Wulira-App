# Wulira App

Hey there! I'm Paul Sentongo, and I built this app called Wulira to help people learn the Luganda language. If you're a tourist visiting Uganda, a resident wanting to connect more with the local culture, or just someone curious about languages, this app is for you. Luganda is one of the most spoken languages in Uganda, and I wanted to make it easy and fun for everyone to pick it up.

## What is Wulira?

Wulira is a mobile app designed to teach Luganda in a simple, interactive way. Whether you're traveling to Uganda and want to say "hello" or "thank you" in the local tongue, or you're living here and want to deepen your understanding of the language, Wulira has you covered. It's not just for tourists—anyone can use it to learn Luganda at their own pace.

The name "Wulira" comes from Luganda, meaning something like "to learn" or "to understand," which fits perfectly with the app's goal.

## Why I Built This App

As someone who grew up in Uganda, I've seen how language can bridge gaps between people. Tourists often struggle to communicate, and locals appreciate when visitors make an effort to learn a bit of Luganda. I thought, why not create an app that makes this easy? I wanted it to be accessible, free to start, and engaging so that learning feels like a game rather than a chore.

I developed Wulira using Flutter, a framework that lets me build the app for both Android and iOS from a single codebase. This way, more people can use it on their phones without hassle. The app includes audio pronunciations, quizzes, flashcards, and lessons that build on each other, so you can track your progress and feel accomplished as you go.

## Features of the App

- **Lessons**: Step-by-step guides to learn basic phrases, vocabulary, and grammar. Start with greetings and build up to conversations.
- **Quizzes**: Test your knowledge with fun quizzes that reinforce what you've learned.
- **Flashcards**: Quick reviews of words and phrases, perfect for on-the-go learning.
- **Progress Tracking**: See how far you've come and what you need to work on next.
- **Audio Support**: Hear how words are pronounced by native speakers to get the accent right.
- **Premium Content**: Unlock advanced lessons and features with a small in-app purchase.
- **Themes**: Switch between light and dark modes for a comfortable experience.

The app is built with user experience in mind—it's intuitive, with a clean design that doesn't overwhelm you. I focused on making sure it's offline-friendly, so you can learn even without internet access.

## How I Developed It

Building Wulira was a journey. I started with the idea during a conversation with some friends about cultural exchange. I sketched out the features on paper, thinking about what would make learning Luganda enjoyable.

I chose Flutter because I'm familiar with Dart, and it allows me to create beautiful, responsive apps quickly. The development process involved:

1. **Planning**: Outlining the app's structure, screens, and data models.
2. **Coding the Core**: Building the main screens like the home page, lesson viewer, and quiz interface.
3. **Adding Data**: Compiling a dataset of Luganda words, phrases, and lessons. I drew from my knowledge and some research to ensure accuracy.
4. **Integrating Features**: Adding audio playback, progress saving, and theme switching.
5. **Testing**: Running the app on different devices to make sure everything works smoothly.
6. **Polishing**: Refining the UI, fixing bugs, and adding the finishing touches.

I used tools like Android Studio for development and Git for version control. The app uses local storage to save your progress, so no data is sent to servers unless you choose to.
